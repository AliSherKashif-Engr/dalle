What is Inpainting?
Inpainting is a powerful technique that allows you to edit specific parts of an image while keeping the rest intact. It's like having a magic paintbrush that understands what you want to create.
How Does Inpainting Differ from Traditional Editing?
Unlike traditional editing tools where you're manually painting or cloning, inpainting with DALL-E 3 uses AI to understand the context of your image and generate new content that fits seamlessly. It's not just copying pixels; it's creating new, contextually appropriate elements.
However, it is important to note that the current version of DALL-E 3 integrated into ChatGPT can't directly edit uploaded images. Instead, it can edit the images it has generated. To edit uploaded images, you can use the separate version available on the OpenArt website (openart.ai). Editing instructions are the same for both versions.

Subject Positioning and Photography Rules
Inpainting in DALL-E isn't just about editing content; it's also a powerful tool for improving composition. For example, by applying classic photography rules like the Rule of Thirds or the Golden Ratio, you can enhance the visual appeal of your images. DALL-E can reposition subjects, adjust framing, or add elements to create more balanced and engaging compositions. This feature is particularly useful for optimizing images for different platforms or improving the focal point of a scene.
